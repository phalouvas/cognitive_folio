Financial Data Sources - Global Stock Exchanges & Regulators

A comprehensive guide to finding financial reports (10-Q, 10-K, Annual Reports, Quarterly Reports) for securities worldwide.

United States

SEC EDGAR (Official)

  • Website: https://www.sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch.html
  • Filings: 10-Q (Quarterly), 10-K (Annual), 8-K (Current Events)
  • Format: PDF and HTML versions available
  • Language: English
  • Coverage: All US publicly traded companies

Quick Access by Ticker

  • Search by Company: SEC Company Search
  • Example - Apple (AAPL):
    • 10-K Filings: Apple 10-K
    • 10-Q Filings: Apple 10-Q

Tips for Finding Reports

Best Practices

  1. Start with Company IR Pages - Usually cleaner PDFs than regulatory filings, better formatted for parsing
  2. For US Stocks - SEC EDGAR is the gold standard; 10-Q (quarterly) and 10-K (annual) are standardized
  3. For International Stocks - Check if company has ADR (American Depositary Receipt) - they file with SEC too
  4. Report Types by Region:
    • US: 10-Q (quarterly), 10-K (annual)
    • Europe: Interim Report/Half-Year Report (semi-annual), Annual Report
    • Asia: Varies - often quarterly or semi-annual + annual
  5. Format Considerations:
    • PDFs are best for parsing (structured tables)
    • Avoid HTML versions (inconsistent formatting)
    • Recent filings (2023+) have more consistent formats

Testing the PDF Parser (Task 3.2)

Recommended Test Order:

  1. US 10-Q (easiest) - Apple, Microsoft, Amazon
  2. Hong Kong Reports - HSBC, Tencent (excellent English)
  3. European Blue Chips - ASML, SAP, Siemens (clean English)
  4. Japanese ADRs - Sony, Toyota (dual SEC/Japan filings)

What to Look For:

  • Income Statement (P&L)
  • Balance Sheet
  • Cash Flow Statement
  • Tables with consistent row/column structure

ADR Listings (File with SEC + Home Country)

Companies with ADRs file both with SEC and their home regulator:

  • Sony (SNE) - Japan/US
  • Toyota (TM) - Japan/US
  • ASML (ASML) - Netherlands/US
  • SAP (SAP) - Germany/US
  • Alibaba (BABA) - China/US
  • Baidu (BIDU) - China/US
  • Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) - Taiwan/US

Advantage: English reports available via SEC EDGAR with standardized US format.

IFRS vs US GAAP

Important: Non-US companies often use IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) instead of US GAAP.

Key Differences:

  • Similar overall structure (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
  • Some terminology differences:
    • "Turnover" vs "Revenue"
    • "Profit" vs "Net Income"
    • "Trade Receivables" vs "Accounts Receivable"
  • PDF parser should handle both with flexible label matching
